# Why Med Spas Lose 60% of Local Traffic (And How to Fix It)

Most med spa owners think their website is "good enough" because it looks professional. But if you're not showing up in the "Local Pack" (the map results) or the top 3 organic results, you're losing **60% or more of your potential local traffic** to competitors who are.

Here are the three silent killers of med spa traffic—and exactly how to fix them.

## 1. Missing Local Schema Markup (The "Invisible" Problem)

Google doesn't "see" your website like a human does. It reads code. If you don't have **LocalBusiness** and **Review** schema markup, Google has no way to know:
* That you are a legitimate medical practice.
* Your exact service area.
* Your star ratings and review count.

**The Result:** Your competitor shows up with **stars and prices** in the search results (rich snippets). You show up as plain text. Guess who gets the click?

**The Fix:**
Implement `LocalBusiness` schema with `medicalBusiness` subtype, plus `Review` and `Service` schema for every treatment page. This tells Google exactly what you are and boosts your CTR by 30%+.

## 2. Thin Service Pages (The "Content Gap")

Many med spas have service pages like "Botox" or "Fillers" that are just 2-3 paragraphs long. Google considers this "thin content" and won't rank it.

**The Reality:**
Top-ranking med spa pages are **800+ words**. They include:
* Detailed procedure descriptions.
* Before/after galleries.
* FAQ sections (answering patient questions).
* Location-specific keywords (e.g., "Botox in [City]").

**The Fix:**
Expand every service page. Add a "What to Expect," "Recovery Time," and "Cost Factors" section. Target long-tail questions patients actually ask.

## 3. Stagnant Review Velocity (The "Trust Gap")

Having 50 reviews is great. But having 50 reviews from **2 years ago** looks suspicious. Google favors practices with **consistent, recent reviews**.

**The Data:**
Practices with 5+ new reviews per month rank **2x higher** than those with 0 new reviews in 90 days.

**The Fix:**
Automate your review requests. Send a text/email 24 hours after every appointment. Make it frictionless.

## The Bottom Line

You don't need a $10,000 website overhaul. You need **technical precision**.

If you're losing traffic to competitors who look *worse* than you, it's likely one of these three issues.
