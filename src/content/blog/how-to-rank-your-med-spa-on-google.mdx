Most med spas are invisible on Google. They have a beautiful website, great before-and-after photos, maybe even a few five-star reviews. But when a potential patient searches "Botox near me" or "best med spa in [city]," they are nowhere to be found.

The practices that do appear? They are not necessarily better at injections. They are better at SEO.

Here is exactly how to fix that.

## Why Med Spa SEO Is Different

Medical aesthetics is one of the most competitive local search verticals. You are competing against established dermatology practices, plastic surgery clinics, franchise med spas, and a growing wave of independent injectors who all want the same local patients.

Generic SEO advice does not cut it here. Med spa SEO requires understanding three things:

- **YMYL signals** — Google classifies health and beauty services as "Your Money or Your Life" content, meaning it demands higher trust signals
- **Local intent** — The vast majority of med spa searches have local intent, which means Google Business Profile and local pack rankings are critical
- **Service-specific keywords** — Patients search for specific treatments, not generic terms, so your content must match that specificity

## Step 1: Nail Your Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is the single most important ranking factor for local med spa searches. Over 40% of clicks on local search results go to the map pack, and your GBP is what determines whether you appear there.

Here is what an optimized GBP looks like:

- **Primary category:** Medical Spa
- **Secondary categories:** Skin Care Clinic, Laser Hair Removal Service, Botox Injection Service
- **Complete business information:** Name, address, phone, website, hours, appointment link
- **High-quality photos:** At least 15 to 20 photos including your facility, team, and treatment rooms (no before-and-after photos, as Google restricts medical images)
- **Regular posts:** Weekly GBP posts about promotions, new treatments, or seasonal specials
- **Review management:** Actively requesting reviews and responding to every single one

The practices that rank in the local pack almost always have more reviews, higher ratings, and more complete profiles than their competitors.

## Step 2: Build Treatment-Specific Service Pages

One of the biggest mistakes med spas make is cramming all their services onto a single page. Google wants to rank pages that are highly relevant to the specific query, so each major treatment should have its own dedicated page.

Your service pages should cover:

- **What the treatment is** and how it works
- **Who is a good candidate** for the procedure
- **What to expect** during and after treatment
- **How long results last** and typical treatment frequency
- **Your pricing structure** or at least a price range
- **Frequently asked questions** specific to that treatment

A well-built Botox service page should be 800 to 1,500 words and answer every question a prospective patient might have. This is not fluff — it is E-E-A-T in action. Google rewards pages that demonstrate expertise, experience, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness.

## Step 3: Create a Content Strategy That Targets Real Searches

Blog content is where you expand your keyword footprint beyond your core service pages. But not just any content — you need to write about what patients are actually searching for.

High-value blog topics for med spas include:

- Treatment comparisons ("Botox vs Dysport: Which is better?")
- Cost guides ("How much does laser hair removal cost in [city]?")
- Aftercare guides ("What to do after a chemical peel")
- Seasonal content ("Best med spa treatments for summer")
- Myth-busting articles ("Does microneedling really work for acne scars?")

Each post should target a specific keyword cluster and include internal links back to your relevant service pages. This creates a content ecosystem that reinforces your topical authority.

## Step 4: Optimize for Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)

Google is no longer the only search engine that matters. AI-powered search tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews are changing how patients discover med spas.

To appear in AI search results:

- **Structure content with clear headings** — AI models parse well-organized content more effectively
- **Answer questions directly** — Start sections with direct, concise answers before elaborating
- **Use schema markup** — JSON-LD structured data helps search engines understand your content type and context
- **Create FAQ sections** — Question-and-answer format aligns perfectly with how AI search queries work
- **Maintain factual accuracy** — AI models prioritize authoritative, well-sourced content

The practices that optimize for both traditional SEO and AEO will capture traffic from every search channel, not just Google.

## Step 5: Technical SEO Foundations

All of the content strategy in the world will not matter if your website has technical issues that prevent Google from crawling and indexing it properly.

Critical technical checks for med spas:

- **Page speed** — Core Web Vitals must be in the green zone, especially on mobile
- **Mobile responsiveness** — All pages must function perfectly on phones, where most med spa searches happen
- **SSL certificate** — HTTPS is non-negotiable for medical websites
- **Canonical tags** — Prevent duplicate content issues across similar service pages
- **Structured data** — JSON-LD schema for LocalBusiness, MedicalBusiness, and Service types
- **XML sitemap** — Submitted to Google Search Console and kept current
- **Internal linking** — Every page should be reachable within three clicks from the homepage

## The Bottom Line

Ranking your med spa on Google is not magic. It is a systematic process of optimizing your local presence, creating treatment-specific content, maintaining technical health, and adapting to the rise of AI search.

The med spas that invest in SEO today will own their local market for years to come. The ones that do not will keep paying more and more for ads while their competitors collect organic traffic for free.
