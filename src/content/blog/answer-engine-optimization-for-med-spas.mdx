The way patients find med spas is changing faster than most practice owners realize. A growing number of people are skipping Google entirely and asking AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI Overviews for recommendations.

When someone types "best med spa for lip filler in Austin" into Perplexity, the AI does not show ten blue links. It gives a direct, synthesized answer — often naming specific practices and explaining why it recommends them.

If your med spa is not in those answers, you are invisible to an entirely new category of patients.

## Traditional SEO vs. Answer Engine Optimization

Traditional SEO focuses on ranking web pages in a search engine results page (SERP). You optimize for keywords, build backlinks, and earn a spot in the top ten results.

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) focuses on getting your practice mentioned in AI-generated answers. The mechanics are fundamentally different:

| Traditional SEO | Answer Engine Optimization |
|---|---|
| Optimize for keyword ranking positions | Optimize for AI citation and recommendation |
| Target Google's algorithm | Target multiple AI models simultaneously |
| Success = page 1 ranking | Success = being named in AI answers |
| Link-based authority | Content-based authority and structured data |
| Results page with 10 links | Single synthesized answer with sources |

The key difference is that AEO is about being the answer, not just ranking near it. AI tools pull from websites that provide clear, authoritative, well-structured information.

## How AI Search Tools Choose Which Practices to Recommend

Understanding how AI search works helps you optimize for it. When a user asks an AI tool about med spas, the system typically:

1. **Interprets the query** — Understands what the user is actually looking for
2. **Retrieves relevant content** — Pulls from its training data and real-time web sources
3. **Evaluates authority** — Weighs which sources are most trustworthy and relevant
4. **Synthesizes an answer** — Combines information from multiple sources into a response
5. **Cites sources** — Links back to the most relevant pages it used

Your goal is to be one of the sources that AI tools consistently pull from when answering med spa-related queries.

## Six AEO Strategies for Med Spas

### 1. Structure Content for Machine Readability

AI tools parse content differently than humans. They extract information most effectively from well-structured pages with clear hierarchies.

Every service page and blog post should use:

- **Descriptive H2 and H3 headings** that match common questions
- **Direct answers in the first sentence** under each heading before elaborating
- **Bullet points and numbered lists** for step-by-step information
- **Consistent formatting** that makes key facts easy to extract
- **Summary sections** that condense the main points

Think of it this way: if an AI tool scanned your Botox page, could it quickly extract what Botox is, who it is for, how long it lasts, what it costs, and why your practice is a good choice? If the answer is not obvious from the structure alone, the page needs work.

### 2. Implement Comprehensive Schema Markup

JSON-LD structured data is the language search engines and AI tools use to understand your content at scale. For med spas, implement these schema types:

- **LocalBusiness or MedicalBusiness** — Your practice details, location, hours, contact
- **Service** — Each treatment you offer with descriptions and pricing
- **BlogPosting** — Your blog content with author, date, and topic information
- **BreadcrumbList** — Your site navigation hierarchy
- **Organization** — Your company entity with founders and expertise areas

Schema markup does not appear on your website — it is code that runs behind the scenes. But it has an outsized impact on how AI tools interpret and cite your content.

### 3. Create Authoritative Question-and-Answer Content

AI tools are fundamentally question-answering machines. The more questions your website answers authoritatively, the more likely you are to be cited.

High-value question content for med spas:

- "How much does [treatment] cost in [city]?"
- "What is the difference between [treatment A] and [treatment B]?"
- "How long does [treatment] last?"
- "What are the side effects of [treatment]?"
- "How do I choose a med spa for [treatment]?"

Each answer should be direct, factual, and backed by your clinical expertise. Avoid vague marketing language. AI tools prioritize content that provides specific, useful information over content that is promotional.

### 4. Build Topical Authority Through Content Clusters

AI tools assess topical authority — how deeply and comprehensively a website covers a subject area. A single page about Botox will not establish you as an authority. But ten interconnected pages covering Botox from every angle will.

A content cluster for Botox might include:

- Main service page: "Botox Treatments at [Practice Name]"
- Blog: "Botox vs Dysport: Which Is Right for You?"
- Blog: "How Long Does Botox Really Last?"
- Blog: "Your First Botox Appointment: What to Expect"
- Blog: "Botox for Migraines: Does It Work?"
- Blog: "Botox Aftercare: What to Do and Avoid"

Each piece links to the others and back to the main service page, creating a web of related content that signals deep expertise to both Google and AI search tools.

### 5. Maintain a llms.txt File

The llms.txt standard is an emerging convention that helps AI crawlers understand your website. Similar to how robots.txt tells search engine crawlers what to index, llms.txt tells AI models who you are and what you do.

A llms.txt file for a med spa should include:

- Company name and one-line description
- Core services offered
- Location and service area
- Key differentiators
- Links to main pages

Place this file at the root of your website (yourdomain.com/llms.txt). While not all AI tools use it yet, adoption is growing and it positions you ahead of competitors who have not implemented it.

### 6. Monitor Your AI Search Presence

You cannot optimize what you do not measure. Regularly test how AI tools respond to queries related to your practice:

- Search your practice name in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews
- Search your key services + city in each tool
- Search comparison queries like "best med spa in [city]"
- Track which competitors are being recommended instead

Document what you find and adjust your content strategy based on gaps. If Perplexity recommends a competitor for "lip filler [city]" but not you, examine what content they have that you are missing.

## The AEO Advantage for Early Adopters

Most med spas have not even heard of Answer Engine Optimization. The practices that start optimizing for AI search now will have a significant first-mover advantage.

AI search usage is growing exponentially. More patients are asking ChatGPT for recommendations before booking appointments. More are using Perplexity to compare treatment options. More are relying on Google AI Overviews instead of scrolling through traditional results.

The practices that show up in these AI answers will capture a growing share of high-intent patients. The ones that do not will wonder why their organic traffic is declining even though their Google rankings have not changed.

AEO is not a replacement for traditional SEO — it is an extension of it. The same content quality, structured data, and topical authority that help you rank on Google also help you appear in AI search results. The difference is in how you structure and present that content.

Start now. Your competitors probably have not.
